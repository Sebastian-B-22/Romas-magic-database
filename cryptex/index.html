The language of computers! Everything becomes 0s and 1s. Each letter is represented by 8 bits (binary digits).

üî¢ Hexadecimal (Hex) Medium

Modern/Digital

How It Works

Base-16 number system used in computing. Uses 0-9 and A-F. Two hex digits = one byte = one character!

HI in hex:

A=41, B=42, C=43... Z=5A (using ASCII values)

üí° How to Crack It:

  • Often shown with "0x" prefix (0x48)

üí° How to Crack It:

  • Without the book, nearly impossible!
